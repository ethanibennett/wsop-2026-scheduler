# Tournament Day Protocol
*Applies year-round · fewer than cash days · within driving distance of Philly · played selectively*

Tournament days don't fit the cash rhythm — fixed early start, no end time you control, 10–14 hours long, a drive on both ends, and sometimes a multi-day commitment. So they're treated as **planned exceptions wrapped in buffers**, not part of the daily rhythm. Because they're rare, you protect the day before and the day after each one.

Discipline here is **a filter + a quota.** Criteria decide *whether* an event is worth playing; the quota stops good criteria from quietly becoming too many days.

---

## Part 1 — The selection filter

Run every event through these. **Any one fails → pass.**

- **Bankroll fit** — buy-in within your tournament cap. Tournaments swing far harder than cash, so they need a much deeper buy-in cushion than your cash stakes do. *[Dial — set the exact cap with the bankroll track.]*
- **Structure / value** — deep stacks and slow levels reward your edge; turbos and hypers are crapshoots where edge barely matters. Favor real guarantees, overlay, and soft/recreational fields.
- **Logistics cost** — drive time vs. what the event is worth. One-day or multi-day? Can you sleep in your own bed, or does it need a hotel? A long drive for a small, fast event is an automatic no.
- **Schedule fit** — does it blow up a protected stretch (a genuine free gap with Ellie, a key cash run, a needed recovery day)? Stricter around the protected gaps (holidays, spring breaks); more latitude during rotation weeks and the grind season.
- **Under quota** — are you below your tournament-days-per-month cap? *[Dial — set the monthly cap; it protects the cash foundation and the sleep rhythm.]*

A "yes" clears bankroll **and** structure **and** logistics **and** schedule, **and** you're under quota. That conjunction *is* the discipline.

---

## Part 2 — The day itself

### Day before (buffer)
- **Bank sleep** — no late cash session the night before. Arrive rested.
- **Prep** — bag, water, real snacks, route, registration, confirmed start time, and your latest acceptable late-reg decided in advance.

### Tournament day
- **Wake** — earlier than the 10:00 anchor, set to (start time − drive − buffer). This is the one day the wake anchor flexes *by design.* Plan the exact time the night before.
- **Compressed first hour** — even rushed, still get daylight + protein + water. Don't drive in unfueled.
- **In-event fueling** *(the health discipline that matters most here)* — hydrate steadily; eat real food on breaks but avoid the heavy dinner-break crash; walk on breaks to break up 12 hours of sitting. Caffeine cutoff is suspended today — but know it'll cost tonight's sleep, which is exactly why the recovery day exists.
- **No cap** — you play till you bust or bag. Accept it going in.
- **Variance** — busting after hours in is a specific gut-punch. Have your reset ready, and guard against the revenge move: do **not** drive to a cash game to "get it back" after a bust.
- **The drive home** *(the real safety issue)* — after 12+ hours and a 2am bust, an exhausted long drive is genuinely dangerous. Decide the rule in advance: if it's far or very late, take a hotel; caffeine for the road; and if you're too gone, don't drive. This isn't optional discipline.

### Day after (recovery)
- The rhythm took a hit — re-anchor, don't pretend it didn't. Hold the 10:00 wake as close as you can, repay debt with an earlier bed that night, light movement, hydrate, **no late cash session** that night. Back in normal rhythm by the following day.
- **If you bagged:** Day 2 is another tournament day — re-run the protocol, and recovery shifts to after the run ends.

---

## How it fits the year

- **Phase 3 (home season):** selective around the protected gaps (the holidays especially) — but the VA and Philly rotation weeks are grind-available, so latitude loosens there.
- **Phase 4 (grind season):** most latitude — you're away and building volume.
- **Buy-in cap and monthly quota** get their real numbers when we build the bankroll track. This is the clearest place health and bankroll meet: every tournament day is a withdrawal from both the roll *and* the rhythm, which is exactly why selectivity matters.
