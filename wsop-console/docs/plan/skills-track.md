# Skills Track — WSOP 2027 Cycle
*Edge × volume = accumulation rate. Skills work is a direct multiplier on the bankroll engine — and the path to the real goal.*

## The north star

Bankroll sorted → **proper bracelet hunter**, building toward a **WSOP Player of the Year** run. POY rewards exactly what this plan produces: **breadth** (a threat in every format), **volume** (a broad slate of events), and **endurance** (deep runs across a long series). Health funds the stamina, bankroll funds the slate, skills make you dangerous in all of it.

Honest calibration: POY is one of the hardest achievements in poker — a multi-year arc that takes a monster series and variance breaking your way. This year's job is narrower and real: become a *legitimate threat in every format you play.* That's the foundation a POY run stands on.

## Two jobs

1. **Widen the edge where you already earn** — PLO first (your biggest study moneymaker), then PLO8 and your strong mixed games. Compounds across your highest-volume games.
2. **Close the leaks that keep you from winning** — you have a solid mixed-game foundation; the work is converting "competent" into "threat to win any tournament" by systematizing heuristics and killing the remaining mistakes.

## The pattern to fix first

You have the resources and you're not using them — coaches you don't book, a course you've stalled on twice. Same shape as the health habits: the constraint isn't knowledge, it's consistent execution. So the fix is the same — schedule it, make it a floor, track it in the Sunday review. The skills work rides on the health track's habit structure (study blocks live in your daytime before evening cash — see the Phase 1 daily plan).

## The three workstreams

**1. Coaching — start using what you have.**
The fastest leak-closer available to you, and you're leaving it on the table. Build a *cadence*, not a one-off: a standing session (weekly or biweekly), with prep (bring flagged hands/spots) and follow-up (every session feeds the library below). Highest ROI in your B-games, but worth it in PLO too.

**2. PLO — finish the course, drill the ranges.**
Your biggest moneymaker, so the highest-ROI study project:
- **Complete Advanced PLO Mastery** with a structure this time so it doesn't stall a third time: fixed study blocks, a per-week module target, a completion deadline (aim: done within Phase 1–3). Track % complete in the Sunday review.
- **Memorize preflop ranges** as a *daily* floor — a range trainer / spaced repetition, small and repeatable like the movement floor. Accuracy % is your metric.

**3. Mixed games — build the heuristics library.**
Turn the foundation into a referenceable system:
- A living **library** organized by family (stud / draw / Omaha) — heuristics, spot rules, and your own leak notes.
- Populate it from coaching, hand review, flagged spots, and **your own solvers as they come online** (workstream 4).
- The families collapse the work: master stud (stud/stud8/razz), draw (2-7 TD/SD), and Omaha (PLO/PLO8/Big O), plus basic LHE — and HORSE + 8-game fall out of the components. Most of your non-NLHE slate is the same handful of family skills.

**4. Proprietary solvers — the edge multiplier (side build).**
Building solvers for games that lack them is the highest-ceiling, most durable edge in the plan: solver-grounded play in formats where the field runs on feel. Outputs feed straight into the library above.
- **Phase-fit:** front-load the build into the low-play windows — P1 Foundation and the calmer stretches of P3 (the holiday gap). Build under easy conditions, deploy the edge during the grind + WSOP — no dev work mid-grind.
- **Time discipline:** it's a fourth claim on the same hours (play / study / coaching / dev) and the most engaging one — the exact risk given the stalled-course pattern. Don't let it crowd out the certain near-term money (volume, finishing the course, booking coaches). **The base earns; the solvers multiply. Keep that order.**

## The convergence (still the highest-leverage habit)

A structured **online grind** accumulates in your edge games, builds reps in your B-games, smooths variance, and generates the **database** you study and build the library from. One habit, three tracks. It's underused right now — building it is the biggest single move available.

## Phase mapping

- **P1 — Foundation:** baseline per-format competence; set up tools (tracker/DB, range trainer, library); **book the first coaching sessions and lock the cadence**; start Advanced PLO Mastery + daily range drills + first online reps. Study-heavy (low live volume).
- **P2 — Monterey (solo):** first real online rep volume; coaching cadence holds; keep grinding the course.
- **P3 — Home season:** still your biggest pre-grind development phase — finish the course, push mixed-game leaks with coaching, grow the library — but balanced against real volume now, since the VA and Philly rotation weeks are grind-available. The deep study/dev concentrates in the calmer stretches (the holiday gap especially).
- **P4 — Grind (solo, Feb–May):** play-heavy; B-games at competence by the start; sharpen under volume with tight leak review.
- **P5 — WSOP:** execution — trust the prep, light review between events. The bracelet hunt begins.
- **P6 — Landing:** re-rate per format, review growth, set next cycle's POY-track targets.

## Measuring it

- **Course % complete** and **preflop range accuracy** (trainer score) — the two hard PLO numbers.
- **Coaching sessions logged** (cadence held?) and **coach assessment** of leak-closure.
- **Library growth** — entries added per phase.
- **Per-format self-rating** (soft → competent → threat), re-rated each phase boundary.
