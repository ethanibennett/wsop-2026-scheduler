# Phase 1 Playbook — Foundation & Reset
*Jul 21 – Sep 21, 2026 · 9 weeks · Ellie in school (pre-rotation) — packed weekdays; genuinely free only in the break before Monterey · low volume · the install phase*

The whole job of Phase 1 is to **install the system while conditions are easiest** — you're home, there's no travel or rotation pressure, and volume is meant to be low (Ellie's in school, busy most weeknights, so your grind has room) — so that by the time the solo grind starts in Monterey, the rhythm runs without willpower. Nothing here is about results. It's about building the machine.

The nine weeks have a clear shape:

> **land + re-entry** (W1) → **install** (W2–3) → **repair** (W4) → **recover & rebuild** (W5–6) → **humming** (W7) → **solo dress-rehearsal** (W8) → **close & hand off to Monterey** (W9)

Two hard anchors everything else flexes around:

- **◆ Borgata soft re-entry — W1 (Jul 21–26).** A few cheap mixed events as a rust-knocker, not a grind.
- **◆ Hernia repair — ~W4 (early–mid Aug, ~2 weeks after the series).** Asymptomatic, so it's a scheduling item; slot it where it fits. Exact date is your surgeon's call.

This doc supersedes the older `phase-1-daily-plan.md` and the Phase 1 section of `weekly-breakdown.md` — it folds both in and brings them current.

# Part 2 · Day by day

The week-by-week says *what* installs when. This is *how a day runs* while it does.

## The dials (the three that hold everything)
- **Wake anchor — 10:00.** Held within ±1 hr even after a rough night. The keystone; everything else hangs off it.
- **Session cap — 1:30.** Last hand. Wind-down 1:30–2:00, in bed by 2:00. The cap exists to protect the anchor.
- **Caffeine cutoff — 6:00pm.** ~8 hrs before sleep. The tight one — it'll rub against evening sessions in weeks 1–3. Sleep-protective on purpose.

## The four fixed points (hold every day, regardless of poker)
1. **Wake + first hour (10:00–11:00)** — daylight (~10 min) → 5–10 min movement → protein breakfast + water. Circadian anchor, movement seed, nutrition default, one block.
2. **Movement floor (by ~4:30pm)** — a real walk; the strength set on lifting days. *Before* the evening, not after.
3. **Caffeine cutoff (6:00pm).**
4. **Wind-down (1:30–2:00am)** — no hand review, dim lights, shower, two-line session journal. In bed by 2:00.

## Day-types

Phase 1 isn't one repeating day — it has four shapes depending on the week.

**1 · Standard install day** (most of Phase 1)
The default frame. First hour → daytime carries the install work (study block, range drills, admin setup, a little solver-dev) → movement floor → evening. What the evening *is* depends on the day: a live session on the fixed/flex nights, study or a with-Ellie pocket when she's free, online otherwise. The weekly grid below assigns each one. Wind-down → bed by 2 regardless.

**2 · Borgata tournament day** (W1 only)
Breaks the standard shape. Anchor still fires at 10:00 → drive to AC → event at 1:15pm → play through the evening, later than a cash night → drive back. Use the `tournament-day-protocol.md` buffers. The one rule that survives: **the next morning's anchor still holds at ±1 hr.** One short night is fine; two chasing each other is the thing to avoid — so no back-to-back-to-back if the anchor is slipping.

**3 · Post-op recovery day** (W4 into early W5)
Stripped down. Anchor at 10:00 (gently) → rest, light reading, presence with Ellie → a cleared short walk when you're able → early, easy night. **No training, no strain, no volume pressure.** The only job is healing and keeping the sleep rhythm from drifting. Don't measure these days against the others.

**4 · With-Ellie time** (her pockets + the pre-Monterey break)
Ellie's in school most of the phase, so this isn't fixed weeknights — it's the windows she's actually free: the odd light evening, weekend pockets, and above all the **break before Monterey**, when school's done and she's not yet away. That break is the real together stretch — treat it as the protected window and the send-off. The rest of the phase, partnership is supporting her *through* the school grind, not reserving nights she'd be studying anyway.

**5 · Sunday MTT day** (fixed, from W6)
A different animal from the cash days. Anchor at 10:00 → weekly review ~1:00pm → MTTs from early afternoon. The catch: tournaments ignore the 1:30 cap. Most weeks you're done before it; on a deep run, take the late night — it's a final table — and let Monday absorb it (later wake, lighter blocks). The rule that protects the system: the Sunday exception stays a Sunday exception. It doesn't leak into the cash nights.

# Part 3 · Study & online

## Study — the Mon & Thu blocks

Two deliberate blocks a week, separate from the daily range drills (those run every day, light). **Mon** is the heavy cognitive block — freshest start of the week, so it goes to your edge. **Thu** is review + mixed-game building.

- **Mon — A-game + build (PLO + solver-dev).** Advanced PLO Mastery progress first; solver-dev once the habit's stable.
- **Thu — mixed library + hand review.** Grow the heuristics library by game family, and review the week's hands (yours and your coach's).

Week by week (formal blocks begin W3 — W1 is Borgata, W2 is setup, W4 is surgery):

- **W2 — setup.** Stand up the rails: range trainer, solver environment, course access, the heuristics-library doc (skeleton by family: stud / draw / Omaha). No content yet.
- **W3 — Mon:** PLO Mastery module 1. **Thu:** flesh out the library for your strongest mixed family; book the first coaching session.
- **W4 — surgery.** Light reading only, if anything. No blocks.
- **W5 — Mon:** PLO Mastery module 2. **Thu:** review your online-return hands; first coaching session if you're up for it.
- **W6 — Mon:** PLO module 3 + scope the first solver-dev target (one under-solved game, a minimal first solve). **Thu:** heuristics — stud8 family; coaching review.
- **W7 — Mon:** PLO module 4 + solver-dev build. **Thu:** heuristics — draw family (2-7 triple/single draw, badugi); hand review.
- **W8 — Mon:** PLO module 5 / finish + solver-dev iterate. **Thu:** heuristics — Omaha-8 / Big O + your weakest B-games.
- **W9 — consolidate.** Capture course takeaways into the library; lock the study cadence you'll carry into Monterey; set Phase 2 targets.

*Fill in when you can:* the course's actual module breakdown, your coaching frequency (default: ~every 2 weeks, or as your coach runs), and the solver's first target game.

## Online — the three jobs

Online does three different jobs in Phase 1. Keep them separate so you don't blur *developing* with *earning*.

- **1 · Edge / accumulation (Wed flex).** Where the money is: PLO, PLO8, and the select mixed games you already beat. Played at the stake the roll supports. Real volume, real results.
- **2 · B-game development (Fri flex).** Reps in the formats you're *building* for the WSOP slate — the mixed games you don't yet beat (stud8, razz, triple/single draw, Big O). Low stakes, results don't matter; volume and pattern-learning do. Feeds the Thu library, and vice versa.
- **3 · Sunday MTTs (Sun afternoon, fixed).** The Sunday majors — for value *and* as live-tournament prep for the WSOP slate (mixed and PLO MTTs especially). The one tournament block in an otherwise cash week.

**Sites:** spread across your rooms (WSOP.com, BetRivers, BetMGM, Phenom) by where each game actually runs — WSOP.com's shared liquidity tends to have the deepest mixed and MTT fields; the PA rooms for PLO/PLO8 cash. Confirm the current spread per site; online offerings shift.

**The Sunday/sleep tension, said plainly:** MTTs don't respect the 1:30 cap. Most Sundays you bust before it; a deep run is the one sanctioned exception — take it, and let Monday flex. The failure mode isn't the occasional 4am final table; it's letting Sunday normalize a dead cap the rest of the week.

# Part 4 · Hourly day templates

Three shapes cover almost every Phase 1 day. The session is the easy part — what makes or breaks it is what you build around it: when you eat, when the last caffeine lands, when you move, how you walk in, how you come down. All three anchor to **wake 10:00 / bed 2:00**, and all three assume the daily range drills run underneath. Times are targets, not a stopwatch. The deeper layers live in `tournament-day-protocol.md`, `mental-health-and-game.md`, and `nutrition.md`.

---

## A · Cash grind day — live at Parx (Tue / Sat)

- **10:00** — Wake anchor + first hour: daylight, 5–10 min movement, protein breakfast, water.
- **11:00** — Slow start, coffee (caffeine's fine this early).
- **11:30** — Light block: range drills (~30 min) + a glance at notes. Tue/Sat aren't heavy-study days.
- **13:00** — Lunch, protein-first.
- **14:00** — Open / life / errands. Bank a short nap if you slept short — you're up late tonight.
- **16:00** — Movement floor: a real walk (no strength — that's M/W/F). Done before the night, not after.
- **16:45** — Pre-session meal: protein + slower carbs, nothing heavy or greasy. Eat now so you're not running on casino food at midnight.
- **17:00** — Last caffeine before the 18:00 cutoff (one cup) + prep: a few warm-up spots, set **one process goal** for the night, confirm the game's running, pull the session bankroll per the rules.
- **17:30** — Drive to Parx (~40 min). Quiet drive, get into headspace.
- **18:15** — Sit. Session on.
- **in-session** — Stand, walk, water every ~90 min. A protein snack if you need it. Run the mental-game checks: table/seat selection (leave a bad one), tilt-reset between big pots, "am I still on my A-game?" The back half is caffeine-free by design — it's what lets you sleep afterward.
- **~12:45–1:00** — **Live cap: rack up.** You're 40 min out, so leaving by ~1:00 is what keeps bed-by-2 real. Cash isn't an MTT — you can always leave; an exceptional game is the only reason to stretch it, and that's a conscious exception, not a habit.
- **1:00–1:40** — Drive home = your wind-down. Let the session settle; don't replay every pot.
- **1:40–2:00** — Quick reset: shower, two-line journal (one thing that worked, one to fix), no hand review. Bed by 2:00.

*Saturday note:* hold this cap hardest on Sat — Sunday is the MTT grind, and two long nights chasing each other is the one thing the system can't absorb.

# Part 5 · Morning & evening routines

Part 4 is the *shape* of each day; these are the **bookends** — the wake ritual and the wind-down — written as repeatable protocols. They're where the system lives or dies: get these two automatic and the middle of the day mostly runs itself.

## The morning anchor — (almost) the same every day

The point of the morning is that it *doesn't* change with the day type. An anchor only anchors if it's invariant — same wake, same first hour, whether last night ended at 2am or early. Run it on autopilot:

1. **10:00 — up on the first alarm.** Feet on the floor, no snooze (snooze fractures the anchor and chops up the last sleep cycle). Phone charges across the room, so killing the alarm means standing up.
2. **Light, immediately.** Blinds open / step outside / 10 min of daylight (a bright lamp on grey mornings). Morning light sets tonight's melatonin timing — it's how you *earn* the 2am bed.
3. **Water.** A big glass before coffee — you wake dehydrated.
4. **Move, 5–10 min.** Mobility flow or an easy walk. Not a workout — just "day's on."
5. **Protein breakfast** (~30–40g) — the nutrition anchor meal.
6. **Then coffee, ~11:00.** Deliberately *after* light + water + food. Delaying the first caffeine 60–90 min past waking blunts the afternoon crash.

The one prohibition: **no inputs before step 5** — no phone-in-bed, email, Discord, news, or results. Inputs hijack the morning and put you on the back foot before you're up. Light and water before screens.

**Small day-type tweaks (only after the first hour):**
- **Grind day (live):** in the open afternoon, bank a **20–30 min nap** if you slept short — hard cap, no 2-hour naps that wreck the night.
- **Sunday MTT:** front-load fuel — bigger breakfast and lunch, since the sit starts at 1:30 and dinner's mid-session. Slate prep replaces the light block.
- **Study day:** the late-morning deep block gets **Do Not Disturb** — your freshest hour goes to the hardest thing.
- **Post-op (W4–5):** same wake / light / water, skip the movement (walking only as cleared), more rest. The anchor holds even when nothing else does.

## The evening wind-downs — differentiated by how the day ends

Mornings are identical; evenings aren't, because the adrenaline and finish time vary wildly. The job every time: down-regulate from play-mode to sleep fast enough to protect the 2am bed. The enemies are screens, adrenaline, and replaying pots.

**E1 · Live-cash nights (Tue / Sat / flex Mon · finish ~1–2am)**
- **The drive home is step one of the wind-down.** Use it — no hype music, let the session settle, don't relitigate hands. The 40 minutes is a gift.
- **~1:40 home:** shower (signals "done," drops core temp for sleep), kill the overhead lights.
- **Two-line journal:** one thing that worked, one to fix. Close it. **No hand review, no solver, no results-refresh** — they re-fire the analytical brain exactly when it needs to go quiet.
- No real late meal (you ate mid-session); if hungry, small and protein only.
- **In bed by 2:00.** Wired? 10 min of breathing / the meditation floor.

**E2 · Sunday MTTs (two endings)**
- **Bust before ~1:30 (most weeks):** run E1 — close tables, shower, two lines, no review, bed by 2.
- **Deep run (the sanctioned exception):** you'll finish late and adrenaline-soaked — *don't* go from the last hand straight to bed, you won't sleep. Build a **longer, deliberate decompress** (20–30 min): shower, low light, breathing or a short walk, let the system come down. Accept the later bed; Monday flexes. And whether it ended in a score or a bustout, **make no decisions** — don't fire up cash to "stay hot," don't spiral a bad beat. Let it settle, then sleep.

**E3 · No-session nights (study days, non-live Mon — the sleep-banking night)**
- The easy one, and quietly the most valuable: no session means you can **bed before 2.** Do it. This is where you repay the debt the grind nights build.
- Books closed by ~17:00 → easy evening (Ellie if she's free) → **screens down 30–45 min before bed** → meditation floor → in bed early.
- The leak to guard: the urge to "use" the free night for more poker or study. Protect it as recovery — that's its whole job.

## The two rules under all of it
- **The 60-minute down-ramp.** Whatever the day, the last hour before bed has no new analytical work and no stimulating inputs. Non-negotiable — it's what makes the 2am bed and the 10am anchor possible.
- **A miss is one day, not a trend.** Blow the wake time or the cap once and the move is to anchor the *next* morning — not write off the week. The system runs on ~80%; what actually breaks it is treating one slip as permission for the second.

**Signs it's working:** asleep within ~20 min of lights-out, waking near 10:00 without an alarm fight, and the mid-afternoon energy crater filling back in. Those three are your dashboard — watch them more than the scale.
