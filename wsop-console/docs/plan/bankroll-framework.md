# Bankroll Framework — WSOP 2027 Cycle
*Everything in buy-ins, not dollars. The post-WSOP-2026 number is the one input you plug in late July.*

## Setup

- **Roll is insulated** — your income covers living costs, so the whole roll is playing money. Standard pro rules, no life-expense padding.
- **PLO/mixed = high variance** → bigger cushions than NLHE guidance.
- **Built in buy-ins**, so it holds whether WSOP 2026 leaves you up, down, or flat. The number only sets which rung you start on.

## Three buckets, kept separate

1. **Cash rebuild roll** — the engine. Grows through your edge games (PLO, PLO8, select mixed).
2. **WSOP 2027 fund** — a separate accumulating bucket fed by a fixed slice of cash profit, targeted to be ready by May.
3. **Mixed-game sub-track** — lives inside the cash roll but counted in *big bets*, since limit variance behaves differently.

## The cash engine — the rules

- **Sit** a stake with **≥ 30 buy-ins** in the cash roll.
- **Move up** only when **both** hold: you have **40 buy-ins of the next** stake, **and** your edge-per-hour is at least as good there. Bankroll permits the move; *accumulation rate* justifies it. If the higher game's field outclasses you, your hourly drops even as the stake rises — don't move up into that.
- **Move down** if you drop below **20 buy-ins** of your current stake. This is the discipline that makes a rebuild work.
- **Take a shot** — when you're fully rolled at your current stake (≥30 bi) *and* hold surplus, you can move up early instead of waiting for the full 40:
  - Earmark **3–5 buy-ins of the higher stake**, drawn only from surplus above your 30-bi current-stake roll — so even a total shot loss leaves you fully rolled where you are.
  - **Stop:** lose the allocation → drop straight back down. No reloading the shot to chase it.
  - **Graduate:** if the shot lifts your roll to **≥30 buy-ins of the higher stake**, you're properly rolled — stay up. That's the win condition.
  - Take shots into **soft** higher games where your edge is real. The shot tests the game, but let the *roll threshold* graduate you, not a small winning sample — variance is too high to read edge off a few sessions.
- 30/40/20 is deliberately conservative because the roll is doing double duty (rebuild **and** feeding the WSOP fund). You can run leaner (~20/15) once the WSOP fund is separately secured.
- **Online** PLO/variants: run a touch more (~50 buy-ins) for the higher hands/hour variance — but it's your best volume + variance-smoothing tool, and you've been underusing it.
- **Mixed (limit) games:** ~300–400 big bets per game you play.

## WSOP 2027 fund

- **Your slate:** every $1.5k non-NLHE (except LHE), every PLO ≤ $10k, every non-NLHE $2.5–3k, and the $10k championships — 2-7 triple draw, 2-7 single draw, stud8, razz, HORSE, **PLO8, Big O, 8-game**, possibly a few more.
- **Face size:** ~$200k max if fired once each, dominated by the $10k championships. Refine when the 2027 schedule releases.
- **The fund target is your NET after sales, not face.** Action-selling is the central lever: it shrinks both the capital you accumulate and your variance.
  - *Worked example:* sell 60% at 1.15 markup → backers cover ~$138k of the ~$200k in entries → **net ~$62k for 40% of the action.** That ~$60–70k is the real fund target.
  - *Tradeoff:* heavy selling caps the ceiling — a big score mostly goes to backers. Right call for a rebuild; just named.
- **Cash-flow ≠ net cost.** You may need to *float* entries before backers settle. Arrange prepayment or keep enough liquid to front events, or you're floating $200k for real.
- **Secure the backing (P3–4 task).** Placing ~$130k of mixed-game action is a narrower market than NLHE — line up staking arrangements before the series, don't assume them.
- **Feed it monthly:** route a fixed slice of cash profit into this bucket across Phases 1–4 so the net target is ready by Phase 5 — don't fund it from the playing roll at the last minute.

- **P6 — Landing:** assess what survived, reset for next cycle.

## The objective

**A-goal: accumulate.** One objective drives everything — grow the roll as fast as your edge allows.

- **Stakes are a throttle, not a destination.** You move up when the roll supports it *and* it raises your accumulation rate — never for its own sake. The target stake is your **EV-per-hour max**, which may well be a soft 5/5/10 over a tough 10/10/25.
- **10/10/25 / 25/25/50 are outputs, not goals.** They happen if and when accumulation carries the roll there with your edge intact. No tension with WSOP — moving up and the slate both serve the one objective.
- **Online is pure rate.** More hands/hour on Phenom mixes that fit your edge is the most underused accumulation lever you have — build it in.
- **WSOP funds out of the accumulated roll** (net ~$60–70k after sales), fed monthly, no separate competition for dollars.

The starting number (late July) sets the opening rung; the engine does the rest.

## Year checkpoints — growing a $50k roll

*These are **roll-level milestones** — what each level unlocks by the rules — with a rough phase overlay. The levels are solid; the **timing** is a target through a variance cloud, not a forecast. You can be at $90k in November and $55k in December and neither means much. Not financial advice — poker income is variable, and the move-down floors below are what keep variance from becoming ruin.*

**The structural engine:** living expenses come from the separate income, so **100% of poker winnings compound into the roll.** That's the whole reason it can grow — most pros bleed the roll to rent. Yours doesn't.

**The growth ladder** (≈30 buy-ins to sit a stake):

|---|---|---|

| $150k+ | 10/10/25 territory — likely next cycle | stretch |

**The downside floors (these matter as much as the ceilings):**
- Under **~$40k** → drop back to 2/2/5 and rebuild. No ego.
- Under **~$25k** → stop, reassess, lean on the separate income while you reset.

The move-down discipline is exactly what makes the aggressive-looking move-up thresholds survivable.

**The one hard target: the WSOP fund.** The ~$200k slate sold to ~$65–70k net means a **~$65k fund built by May**, carved off as its own bucket once the playing roll clears ~$100k — so the campaign never risks the playing roll. If the build runs behind, the lever is selling *more* action, not dipping the roll.

**Post-WSOP is its own swing.** The fund deploys into ~$200k of variance: it can come back near zero (down to your ~$70k playing roll) or multiply on a deep run. Protect the playing roll regardless — the fund is what's at risk, not the engine. Phase 6 tallies what survived and resets.

## Cash volume ramp (hours/week)

You ramp *into* the ceiling — you don't open at it. The body, the roll, and the installed system all have to come online before peak volume is sustainable rather than reckless.

| Phase | Cash hrs/wk | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **P1 — Foundation** | ~10–15 | Low by design — install + the surgery gap (W4 near zero) |
| **P2 — Monterey** | ~20 | First real solo ramp |
| **P3 — Home Season** | ~25 | The big build; grind the rotation weeks |
| **P4 — Grind Season** | ~30 (band 28–32) | Full ramp, Ellie away, everything on accumulation |
| ceiling | ~35 | Only if all five cash nights run long — the redline, not cruising |

**Peak is ~30, not ~45.** Five cash nights (Tue/Sat fixed + Mon/Wed/Fri flex) at ~6 hrs each ≈ 30. The 1am cap + 10am anchor deliberately trade away the 45–50 hrs a "sit till it breaks" grinder logs — longevity over raw hours. The only lever for more is an earlier (afternoon) start, which costs the daytime structure (study, training, gym); not worth it outside rare spots.

**Realistic average < ceiling.** Even in P4, most weeks won't hit all five nights (game quality, energy, a flex day pulled to study) — so plan ~30 as the ceiling, ~25 as the lived average.

**Cash isn't the whole grind.** Add the Sunday MTTs (~8–12 hrs) and peak *total* poker is ~40 hrs/week. The cash ceiling isn't the work ceiling.
