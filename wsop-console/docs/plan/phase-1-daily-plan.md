# Phase 1 — Daily Operating Plan
*Foundation & Reset · late July → Sept 21 · ~8 weeks · Ellie around (off-rotation) · low volume · **mostly cash***

The job of Phase 1 is to install the health system while conditions are easiest, so it runs without willpower before the solo grind. Tuned to your real clock: **wake 10:00, bed by 2:00, mostly evening cash.**

Good news baked in: because you're playing mostly cash on a fixed 2am/10am window, your days settle into *one repeatable rhythm* instead of a mix. A consistent bedtime is exactly what makes a wake anchor stick — so this schedule is friendlier to habit-building than a tournament-driven one would be.

---

## The dials

- **Wake anchor — 10:00am.** Held within ±1 hr *even after a late or rough night.* The keystone.
- **Session cap — 1:30am.** Last hand. Wind-down 1:30–2:00, **in bed by 2:00.** The cap exists to protect the wake anchor.
- **Caffeine cutoff — 6:00pm.** ~8 hrs before sleep. *The tight one* — it'll rub against evening sessions. Sleep-protective on purpose; watch the tension in weeks 1–2.

---

## The daily skeleton — holds every day

Four fixed points that don't move regardless of when (or whether) you play:

1. **Wake anchor + first hour (10:00–11:00).** The one non-negotiable block. In order: daylight (outside or by a window, ~10 min) → 5–10 min movement → protein breakfast + water. Circadian anchor, movement seed, and nutrition default in one block.
2. **Movement floor (by ~4:30pm).** A real walk, plus the short strength set on strength days — done *before* the evening session, not after.
3. **Caffeine cutoff (6:00pm).** Last caffeine of the day.
4. **Wind-down (1:30–2:00am).** 30 min: no hand review, dim lights, shower, a two-line session journal to close it mentally. In bed by 2:00.

Your evening cash session lives between the movement floor and the cap. Everything else flexes around the four points.

---

## Day-types

Mostly cash on a fixed window means most days are the same shape:

| | Evening cash (your default) | Off day (no poker) | Occasional daytime/long session |
|---|---|---|---|
| **First hour** | 10:00 anchor | 10:00 anchor | 10:00 anchor |
| **Daytime** | life / study / movement / meals | same | session may start early |
| **Poker** | evening → cap (1:30) | none | ends in time to still wind down |
| **Wind-down** | 1:30–2:00 | normal | before 2:00 regardless |
| **Sleep** | by 2:00 | by 2:00 — bank it | by 2:00 |

The cap holds the line in all three: however the session goes, you stop by 1:30 and you're in bed by 2:00. One short night is fine; the thing to avoid is two in a row chasing each other.

---

## Weekly shape

- **Strength 3×** (e.g. Mon / Wed / Fri), in the afternoon before sessions; **a walk every day.**
- **One social anchor that isn't Ellie** — gym, a standing call, a friend — at least weekly. This is the seed that has to survive Phase 4, so plant it now while it's easy.
- **Sunday review (10 min):** did the wake anchor hold this week? What slipped? Pick the *one* thing to tighten. (This is what the tracker view will surface later.)
- Aim for **~80%, not perfection.** A missed day isn't a broken streak unless you let it become two.

---

## The 8-week install ramp

Starting from "some habits, inconsistent," the worst move is installing everything at once. Layer one or two things at a time onto a stable anchor:

- **Weeks 1–2 — keystone only.** Just the wake anchor + first hour. Nothing else. Get it to ~80% before adding anything. (This is also when you'll feel the caffeine-cutoff tension — note it, don't fix it yet.)
- **Weeks 3–4 — the sleep bookends.** Add the session cap, the wind-down ritual, and the caffeine cutoff. Full sleep system running.
- **Weeks 5–6 — movement, meals, social.** Add the daily movement floor as its own block, lock the shared dinner with Ellie as a deliberate anchor, seed the weekly non-Ellie social anchor.
- **Weeks 7–8 — full system + dry run.** Add the post-session reset / tilt practice and the Sunday review. Then deliberately run 3–4 days *as if Ellie were away* — the dress rehearsal for Monterey. Whatever wobbles, you've got time to reinforce before Phase 2.
