# Training Plan — WSOP 2027 Cycle
*The how-to-train layer under the health goals. Three modalities, periodized inverse to the poker grind, built around the hernia recovery. A first pass — adjust to your equipment and starting numbers.*

## The shape of it

- **Three modalities, ranked:** strength (the priority — preserve muscle in the deficit and undo the sitting), zone 2 cardio (the aerobic base — drives resting HR and VO2max down/up, low fatigue, survives anything), daily mobility (flexibility + the sitting antidote).
- **Periodized inverse to poker:** BUILD in the calm phases, MAINTAIN through the grind, MINIMAL during WSOP, RECOVER in Landing. You can't build during months of solo grind — so don't try. Bank it in Phases 1–3 and hold it.
- **Consistency over ambition:** floors that survive bad days, three fixed strength days, mobility stacked onto the daily rhythm. ~80%, not perfection.

## The weekly template (build phases)

- **Strength ×3** (e.g. Mon/Wed/Fri, early afternoon before the session) — full-body, ~45 min.
- **Zone 2 cardio ×3–4** — progress the daily walk into structured zone 2 (brisk walk/incline/bike/row), building duration (20–30 min → 45+). Conversational pace.
- **Mobility daily** — ~10 min, stacked into the first hour or the strength warm-up.
- **Floor (worst-day minimum):** the walk + 10 min mobility. Always happens, no matter what.

## Strength — the program

Full-body 3×/week, built on movement *patterns* not body parts, so a missed day never skips anything. Full university gym, so use everything. Pick one per pattern each session and rotate the variations.

- **Squat (knee-dominant):** goblet/front squat (upright — easy on knee + back) · leg press (controllable) · Bulgarian/split squat. Strong quads protect the patellofemoral joint — build gradually, no pain.
- **Hinge (posterior chain — knee insurance):** RDL or trap-bar deadlift (trap bar = the hernia-friendlier loaded hinge) · hip thrust · back extension + leg curl. The last two are low-core-pressure — your early/post-op hinge before heavy deadlifting returns.
- **Push:** DB or machine chest press · push-ups (benchmark) · DB/landmine shoulder press. DB/machine over heavy barbell overhead early (less intra-abdominal pressure).
- **Pull:** pull-ups / lat pulldown (benchmark) · chest-supported or cable row · face pulls (undo the poker hunch).
- **Carry/Core (most hernia-gated — clearance-led):** Pallof press, dead bug, plank to start; loaded carries later. **Avoid crunches/sit-ups and breath-holding strain around the repair.**
- **Prehab every session:** calf raises (straight + bent knee) + foot/ankle work (plantar fascia); glute-med banded walks + step-downs (knee).

2–4 sets, ~6–12 reps, progressive overload — add a little each week. Your benchmarks (push-ups, pull-ups, plank, squat) are the program's spine; you train the tests.

## Cardio — rower first, impact-free intensity

Your history of plantar fasciitis and runner's knee points cleanly at low-impact:

- **Rowing is your primary engine** — zero ground impact, protects both the foot and the knee, great for zone 2 *and* intervals.
- **Incline treadmill walking** is a fine low-impact secondary — supportive shoes, ramp volume slowly.
- **Avoid treadmill running** — highest-risk activity for both past injuries, and unnecessary. Get VO2max intensity from **rower intervals**, not running.
- **Zone 2 is the workhorse** (builds the aerobic base, drops resting HR, low recovery cost — survives the grind). Add intensity only in the calm build phases (1, 3), once healed.
- **Progress volume gradually** — sudden jumps are what trigger both PF and runner's knee. The calf/foot + glute prehab above is your insurance.

Oura shows the payoff: resting HR down, VO2max up.

## Flexibility — daily mobility (load-bearing at 45)

At 45, with 8+ hours in a chair and a PF/knee history, mobility moves from "nice" to injury insurance. The goal is *usable range with control*, not passive stretching.

- **Target your specific tight spots:** hips + hip flexors (sitting shortens them), hamstrings, thoracic spine (rounds from hunching at the table), shoulders/neck (poker posture), ankles/calves/feet (PF), knees (the prehab).
- **Loaded/active, not just static:** deep-squat holds (hips/ankles + the benchmark), controlled articular rotations (CARs) for hips and shoulders, end-range work — at 45, control matters as much as range.
- **Daily small + one longer weekly:** 10–15 min daily (stacked into the first hour, the lifting warm-up, or the wind-down) plus one ~30-min deliberate session a week.
- **Anti-sitting micro-breaks** during play (already in the daily plan): stand and run a couple of openers every break — half the battle for a seated grinder.
- Aimed at the benchmarks (toe-touch, deep-squat hold, shoulder reach) and feeding the prehab (calf/foot for PF, glute-med/step-downs for the knee).

## The hernia ramp (Phase 1)

This drives early training. Surgeon governs the exact timeline — this is the shape, not medical advice:

- **W1 (Borgata):** just the floor — daily walks + mobility. You're re-entering at the tables, not the gym.
- **W2–3 (pre-op):** establish the habit *lightly* — learn the movements, light/supported loads, zone 2 (rower or walks), daily mobility. Groove the routine before surgery interrupts it; don't chase load you'll only have to pause.
- **W4 (surgery):** the repair. No training — rest, then walking only as your surgeon clears it.
- **W5 (early recovery):** walking (easy zone 2) + gentle mobility away from the site. No lifting. Keep the rhythm and aerobic base alive.
- **W6 (return):** reintroduce strength gradually on clearance — rebuild from light, no ego. Rower back, easy.
- **W7–9 (build):** progressive overload begins. Healed and building before Monterey.

## Periodization across the year

| Phase | Training mode |
|---|---|
| **1 · Foundation** | Hernia ramp → build once healed. Install the habit. |
| **2 · Monterey** | Build/maintain — full program holds through the solo ramp. |
| **3 · Home** | **Biggest build** — push strength + add cardio intensity; most of the weight comes off here. Holiday gap = deload/maintain. |

## Phase 1 — the actual sessions

The ramp above says *when*. Here's *what* — the assembled Mon/Wed/Fri session in its two Phase 1 versions, plus how cardio and fuel slot in.

### The strength session (full-body, ~40 min · Mon / Wed / Fri PM)

Same skeleton every time — one movement per pattern, rotate the variations, prehab to close. Two versions in Phase 1:

**Pre-op (W2–3) — light & hernia-safe.** The job is grooving patterns and building the habit, *not* loading. Machines and supported variations, light weight, controlled reps — and per the repair: no heavy bracing, no breath-holding strain, no crunches/sit-ups, no heavy carries.
- Warm-up: 5 min easy rower + mobility (hip / T-spine CARs)
- Squat: goblet squat or leg press — light
- Hinge: back extension or leg curl (low core pressure — skip heavy deadlifts)
- Push: machine or DB chest press (not heavy overhead)
- Pull: lat pulldown or chest-supported row
- Core: Pallof press / dead bug — gentle, no strain (stop at any pull near the site)
- Prehab: calf raises + foot work (PF) · glute-med band walks + step-downs (knee)

**Post-op rebuild (W6 → 9) — from light, progressing.** Same patterns, rebuilt from the bottom on your surgeon's clearance. Reintroduce the trap-bar hinge, goblet/front squat, presses and rows; add a little each week — the progressive overload starts here. **Loaded core and carries come back last** (most hernia-gated, clearance-led, late in the phase).

The bodyweight benchmarks (push-ups, pull-ups, plank, squat hold) are the spine — once healed, you train the tests.

### Cardio — base only in Phase 1
- **The daily walk** is the floor: every day, worst-day minimum.
- **Zone 2 rower, 2–3×/week:** a 10–15 min finisher on strength days + one longer standalone (Thursday or a flex day), building 20 → 40+ min. Pre-op it's fine; post-op, walking first (W5), rower returns easy (W6), build from there.
- **No intensity yet.** Phase 1 is aerobic base. Rower intervals wait until you're healed and built (late W8–9 at the earliest) — real intensity is a Phase 3 job.

### Mobility — daily, non-negotiable
~10 min stacked into the first hour or the lift warm-up, plus one longer weekly session. This is the one modality that never pauses — it runs straight through surgery week (away from the site), the thing that keeps the body-rhythm alive while lifting can't.

### How it lands on the week
- **Strength sits on Mon / Wed / Fri PM** (~16:00, the movement-floor slot) — and notice the design: none of those are your fixed live nights (Tue/Sat) or the Sunday MTT grind. Monday's clean (no poker). **Wed/Fri are the watch-out:** if you go live those nights it's lift *then* Parx — a long day, so lift earlier that afternoon and keep it tight.
- **Fuel it:** lifting ~16:00 dovetails with the ~16:45 pre-session meal on live days — that meal doubles as post-lift protein. Other days, a protein meal or shake in the window after. Creatine daily (supplements section). Post-op, protein stays high to heal — the cut's paused anyway (see `nutrition.md`).

## Phase 1 — the specific lifts

The selection rule for you: **machine- and dumbbell-biased, joint-friendly, posture-correcting, hernia-aware.** Preserving muscle and undoing the chair, not chasing maxes — so the trade of "a little less load, far less injury risk" is right every time. (Loads/clearance per your surgeon; the knee rule is always *no pain*.)

By pattern, top pick first:

**Squat (quads · knee insurance)**
- **Leg press** *(top pick)* — back-supported (low pressure on the repair), controllable depth (kind to the knee), easy to progress safely. Your main quad builder.
- Bulgarian split squat (DBs) — single-leg stability is direct knee insurance; light load, low pressure.
- Goblet squat — upright, knee- and back-friendly; the light pre-op version and a warm-up.
- *Skip early/heavy:* barbell back squat (bracing + deep knee load).

**Hinge (glutes · hams · back — posture + knee)**
- **Hip thrust** *(top pick)* — big glute stimulus, almost no spinal load, low pressure. Safe all phase.
- Seated/lying leg curl — direct hamstring (hamstrings protect the knee), zero core pressure → a pre-op and always-on staple.
- Back extension — low-pressure posterior chain; your pre-op hinge.
- Trap-bar deadlift — the loaded hinge, reintroduced in the *build* block (W7+) once cleared; neutral grip, easier bracing than conventional.
- *Skip early/heavy:* conventional barbell deadlift.

**Push (chest · shoulders)**
- **Machine or DB chest press** *(top pick)* — supported, shoulder-friendly, pre-op-safe.
- Incline DB press — upper chest.
- Landmine or seated DB shoulder press — overhead with far less intra-abdominal pressure than a standing barbell press.
- Push-ups — your bodyweight benchmark (train the test).

**Pull (back · rear delts — your posture priority)**
- **Chest-supported row** *(top pick)* — the chest pad takes the lower back and core out of it (hernia-friendly), pure upper-back, directly un-hunches the poker posture.
- Lat pulldown — builds toward the pull-up benchmark.
- Seated cable row — mid-back.
- **Face pulls — basically every session.** Rear delts + external rotation; the single best antidote to a grinder's rounded shoulders.

**Core / carry (most hernia-gated — clearance-led, comes back LAST)**
- Pre-op + early: Pallof press, dead bug, bird dog — gentle anti-movement core, no spinal flexion, no breath-holding. Stop at any pull near the site.
- Build (post-clearance, late phase): loaded carries (farmer's / suitcase), plank progressions.
- *Never around the repair:* crunches, sit-ups, heavy twists, anything with breath-holding strain.

**Prehab (every session — your injury insurance)**
- **Calf raises** — straight-knee + seated/bent-knee, for the plantar fascia and ankle.
- **Step-downs** (slow, controlled off a low box) + **Spanish squat / banded TKE** — the patellofemoral/VMO work that is *the* runner's-knee insurance.
- **Glute-med** — banded lateral walks, clamshells — hip stability tracks the knee.
- Foot/arch work — short-foot holds, towel scrunches — for the PF.

### A sample week (rotate the variations)

Each day hits every pattern; the variations rotate so nothing gets stale and a missed day skips nothing.

- **Mon:** leg press · hip thrust · machine chest press · chest-supported row · Pallof · calf raises + step-downs + glute-med
- **Wed:** Bulgarian split squat · leg curl + back ext · incline DB press · lat pulldown · dead bug · seated calf + Spanish squat + foot work
- **Fri:** goblet/hack squat · trap-bar deadlift *(build)* / leg curl *(pre-op)* · DB shoulder press · seated cable row + **face pulls** · bird dog · calf raises + step-downs

Pre-op (W2–3): run the light/supported picks, submaximal. Post-op (W6→9): rebuild from light, add a little each week — loaded hinge and carries last.
