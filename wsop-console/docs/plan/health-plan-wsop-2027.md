# Health Plan — WSOP 2027 Cycle

*A living document. Health is the foundation track; bankroll and skills plug in on top and will shift some of this. Built around Ellie's rotation schedule.*

---

## The operating model

Three principles run through every phase:

1. **Sleep/rhythm is the keystone.** All four flagged areas are connected, and rhythm sits upstream — poor sleep wrecks appetite regulation, kills training energy, and tanks stress tolerance. Fix the rhythm and you get partial wins in the other three for free. When in doubt, protect sleep first.

2. **Anchor the wake, not the bedtime.** A mixed play schedule (late cash + daytime tournaments) means sleep onset will always move. So hold a consistent **wake time** and a fixed **first hour** (daylight → light movement → protein), within ~1 hour even after late nights. Pair it with a hard cap on how late and how often you play. That's the whole sleep strategy.

3. **Consistency over ambition.** The constraint is adherence, not knowledge. Everything here is built as small floors, habits stacked onto existing anchors, and structure that survives bad days — not plans that need motivation.

**The standing rule for the hard months:** if you're fried, pull back *poker volume*, not health. Health is the thing that doesn't flex.

**The real axis is free vs. on-rotation, not home vs. away.** When Ellie's on a rotation — in Philly or away — she's slammed, so you're effectively on your own clock and the system has to stand on its own. Her presence only scaffolds your routine in the genuine *gaps* between rotations (the summer, the holidays, the spring breaks); during her rotation weeks her hospital hours may disrupt your rhythm rather than anchor it. The year still runs easy-to-hard, but the easy windows are those gaps, not whole "home" phases.

## Phase 1 — Foundation & Reset
**Late July → Sept 21 · ~8 weeks · Ellie around (off-rotation, genuinely free) · low poker volume**

The install window, and the easiest conditions you'll get all year. Goal: build the system, not grind.

- **Sleep/rhythm (priority):** establish the wake anchor + first-hour routine. Set a latest-session-end time and a ~30-min wind-down after late nights (no hand review, dim light, shower — something that closes the session mentally).
- **Movement:** set an embarrassingly small daily floor — a walk plus a couple of short strength sessions. Build anti-sitting into play (move every break). Win = the streak, not intensity.
- **Nutrition:** install defaults, don't plan meals — a default breakfast, decent session snacks stocked, water at the table, no caffeine within ~8 hrs of target sleep. Shared dinners with Ellie anchor a real mealtime.
- **Stress/social:** short daily decompression + a post-session reset ritual (doubles as tilt control). Critically, seed social anchors that *don't* depend on Ellie (gym, friends, a standing call) so they exist before Phase 4.

**Threat:** trying to fix all four at once. → Rhythm leads; the others are minimum floors.
**End-of-phase check:** wake anchor holding most days, movement streak alive, defaults automatic, social anchors seeded.

## Phase 2 — First Sprint (Monterey)
**Sept 22 → Oct 22 · ~30 days · Ellie away · volume ramps**

The first solo test, in a new city — a low-stakes dress rehearsal for the Phase 4 grind. Add nothing new; just keep the system alive without scaffolding.

- **Sleep/rhythm:** hold the wake anchor despite the new environment and higher volume — use morning daylight aggressively (travel disrupts circadian rhythm). The session-end cap matters more now with no one pulling you off the felt.
- **Movement:** scout a gym / walking route on day one. Portability is the whole test.
- **Nutrition:** the classic collapse point. Grocery run on arrival; lock 2–3 repeatable, easy meals for the month so convenience food isn't the default.
- **Stress/social:** isolation begins — lean on the remote anchors (calls with Ellie + the others). Watch for the solo spiral (play → eat badly → no sleep → tilt); the post-session reset is the circuit breaker.

**Threat:** habits that were propped up by home quietly evaporate. → Pre-commit before leaving. Treat anything that slips as a *diagnostic*, not a failure — note it and reinforce before February.

## Phase 3 — Home Season
**Late Oct → early Feb · ~3.5 months · VA rotation (busy) → holiday gap (free) → Philly rotation (busy)**

Not a low-volume valley. She's slammed on the VA and Philly rotations that bracket it, so those weeks are grind-available; the genuine protect-and-recharge window is the **holiday gap (~Nov 26 – Jan 3)**. So: grind the rotation weeks, go fully present over the holidays. Study/development still fits the calmer stretches, but this is no longer "go light for three months." Health-wise the test is comfort and indulgence (holidays) rather than solitude.

- **Sleep/rhythm:** the long stretch where the rhythm ingrains. Hold the wake anchor through the holidays and any family travel — and don't let her hospital hours during the rotation weeks pull your rhythm around.
- **Movement:** threat is sedentary comfort + Philly winter. Keep the floor; don't lose ground over the holidays.
- **Nutrition:** holiday indulgence and drinking are fine — keep the *defaults* running *around* them so they don't become the new baseline. Maintenance, not restriction.
- **Stress/social:** the holiday gap is your real recharge — bank it, be present. During the rotation weeks, protect the daily touchpoint with Ellie but otherwise treat the time as your own. Honest point to manage alcohol around play and holidays — sustainable moderation, not a ban.

**Threat:** slow erosion and the "I'll restart in January" trap. → Maintenance beats perfection. By the end you've proven the system across solitude (Monterey) *and* home comfort before the real test.

## Phase 4 — Grind Season
**Feb 8 → mid-July · four away blocks (Cleveland ×2 · Long Island · Rockville) · Ellie away · peak volume/stakes**

The payoff phase. Months of solo grind across multiple cities — by now the system must be automatic, because this is where poker players normally fall apart.

- **Sleep/rhythm:** non-negotiable infrastructure now. At higher volume and stakes, sleep *is* performance — protect it ruthlessly. Re-run the Monterey arrival playbook at each new city.
- **Movement:** counters the physical toll of long sitting and doubles as a stress outlet. The floor is what keeps you human across months.
- **Nutrition:** the long-haul risk is months of solo eating. Defaults must be on rails — grocery-run-on-arrival playbook for each new place.
- **Stress/social:** the real danger zone — isolation × variance × duration, where burnout and tilt compound. The anchors from Phase 1 now carry real weight. Use the inter-block gaps when Ellie's home (**3/12–3/29** and **6/4–6/21**) as deliberate recovery pit-stops, not just more grind.

**Threat:** everything tested at once, for months. → It all exists for exactly this. Lean on the gaps, and invoke the standing rule — fried means cut volume, not health.

## Phase 5 — WSOP 2027
**Within the back half · ~late May → mid-July (overlaps Long Island / Rockville) · the series**

Health stops being maintenance and becomes a performance edge. The players who deep-run late are often the ones whose minds and bodies held up.

- **Sleep/rhythm:** the #1 performance variable. Tournament days run long and late — manage sleep like an athlete: as consistent as possible, strategic naps, guard recovery between Day 1s and Day 2s. **Taper in** — arrive rested, not fried from grinding right up to it.
- **Movement:** light only — keep blood moving and circulation up through 12-hour sitting days. Walk between levels. Don't add training load mid-series.
- **Nutrition:** steady fuel for sustained focus — avoid the big dinner-break crash, stay hydrated, time caffeine for the long day without wrecking that night's sleep.
- **Stress/social:** variance is brutal here; the tilt/reset discipline matters most. Have a between-event reset for managing bustouts.

**Threat:** the series tempts you to abandon everything — marathon days, junk, no sleep, drinking. → Health is the edge precisely when everyone else lets it go.

## Phase 6 — Landing
**July 19 → Aug 12 · ~3.5 weeks · Ellie home but on the St. Christopher's rotation (busy)**

Deliberate recovery and honest assessment — don't leave the post-series comedown shapeless. She's slammed on a Philly rotation here, so this is genuinely your recovery/review window rather than a togetherness phase (keep the daily touchpoint).

- **Sleep/rhythm:** repay sleep debt and let the rhythm normalize. Ease down rather than crashing into disorder.
- **Movement:** gentle return; rebuild after the WSOP sitting marathon.
- **Nutrition:** back to the home defaults.
- **Stress/social:** decompress from the year and process the result — win or lose — in a healthy way. Daily touchpoint with Ellie holds, but she's busy, so lean on your own recovery and other anchors.
- **Assess:** review the whole year — what held, what slipped, what carries into the next cycle (which the TBD post-August rotation kicks off).

**Threat:** the post-goal letdown that often follows a big push. → Plan the recovery deliberately.

## Where the other tracks will plug in

Flagging the seams now, since these will adjust the plan:

- **Poker volume (skills/bankroll) trades directly against health load.** The volume ramp through Phases 2 and 4 is the main pressure on sleep, movement, and isolation — the "cut volume, not health" rule is the shared lever.
- **Session-end caps are a joint health + discipline tool** — they protect sleep *and* stop you playing tired/tilted. Bankroll discipline and the wind-down ritual reinforce each other.
- **WSOP bankroll deployment (Phase 5)** sits right on top of the performance-health focus — how much you play and at what stakes will shape how hard the recovery in Phase 6 has to be.
- **The inter-block gaps (3/12–3/29, 6/4–6/21)** are contested real estate — recovery for health, but also potential study/volume time. We'll decide the split when skills and bankroll go in.
