# Mental Health & Mental Game
*The foundation under everything else. Mental health is the base; the mental game is performance built on top — and they feed each other. A high-variance, solo-grind-heavy, ambitious year makes this one of the highest-leverage and most protective threads in the plan, not an afterthought.*

## Two layers, deeply linked

- **Mental health** — the foundation: baseline wellbeing, stress, mood, the loneliness of solo months, the emotional reality of rebuilding after a rough patch, reaching for something big at 45.
- **Mental game** — performance on top: tilt, variance tolerance, downswing resilience, the bracelet-hunt pressure.
- **Not separable:** poor mental health → more tilt, worse decisions, burnout. Brutal downswings → they hammer mental health. Tend the foundation and the performance layer gets easier; ignore it and both crack at the worst time (the Phase 4 grind, a WSOP downswing).

## The pattern — held with care

You have tools that have worked (meditation, hypnotherapy) and aren't using them, and you're considering therapy you haven't started — same shape as the coaches and the course: the constraint is consistent engagement, not access. The scaffolding is the same (schedule it, floor it, review it). But this one isn't just a habit to optimize — give the re-engagement more weight than a checkbox.

## Mental health — the support

- **Therapy as standing infrastructure, not crisis response.** Set it up *before* you need it; a weekly/biweekly cadence is a base you build on, especially heading into the solo grind. BetterHelp/Talkspace suit your schedule; in-person via a directory is another route. Fit matters more than platform — ideally someone who gets high-variance / performance careers rather than someone who frames poker as the problem.
- **Meditation — re-engage as a daily floor.** Already half-built into your day (the decompression, the wind-down). Make it a small, real daily practice; it doubles as tilt control and sleep support.
- **Hypnotherapy — bring it back for specific jobs.** It's worked for you — use it where it fits: performance, sleep, habit installation, downswing reset. Periodic, not daily.

## Mental game — the performance layer

- **Tilt & reset** (already in the plan): the post-session reset ritual, the in-event reset for WSOP bustouts. Make them practiced, not theoretical.
- **Variance & downswings:** the rebuild and the $10k championships are high-variance — expect downswings and have a protocol. Hold the bankroll rules, don't chase, lean on the move-down discipline, separate results from decision quality. A downswing is a math event, not a verdict on you.
- **The rebuild's emotional reality:** climbing back after a rough patch carries impatience, doubt, comparison. Name it — it's normal, and it's exactly where the mental-health foundation earns its keep.
- **Mental-game coaching/study:** a poker mental-game coach (or the established material) is a real edge. Fold it into the skills cadence and add a "what tilted me / what I avoided" line to the Sunday review.

## When the load peaks (phase-aware)

- **Phase 4 (grind):** isolation × variance × duration — the danger zone. Therapy cadence and social anchors must *already be running* by here. The strongest argument for setting it all up in Phase 1.
- **Phase 5 (WSOP):** variance brutality, bustout management, slate pressure — the reset discipline matters most.
- **Any downswing:** the protocol runs regardless of phase.

## Building it in

- **Phase 1:** start therapy, re-establish the daily meditation floor, line up a mental-game resource. Install it while it's calm — same logic as the rest of the plan.
- **Daily:** meditation floor (stacks into the existing decompression/wind-down).
- **Weekly:** therapy cadence; the Sunday review gains a mental-game/mood line.
- **As needed:** hypnotherapy for specific jobs; the reset rituals in the moment.

## Phase 1 — the install plan, week by week

Both layers, staggered like everything else: **foundation first** (it's the base), **performance layer once you're healed and back to volume.** The guiding idea — build these while it's calm, because you can't install a downswing protocol in the middle of a downswing.

A portability note up front: this is the year you spend months away (Monterey, Cleveland, Vegas), so **choose tools that travel.** Remote therapy (BetterHelp/Talkspace) over in-person *specifically* because it survives the rotations; meditation and the reset rituals are portable by nature. Build the version that works in a Cleveland hotel room, not just at home.

- **W1 (Borgata) — notice, don't formalize.** Your first reps back are low-stakes — use them as a quiet test run of the pre-session intention and the post-session two-line journal. No system yet; just pay attention to how it feels to play again.
- **W2 — book the therapy intake.** The single most important mental move of the phase, and the one most easily deferred. Pick the portable option, book the first session, set a standing cadence (weekly to start). Frame the agenda as *infrastructure*: the solo months ahead, results-tied self-worth, the rebuild, the relationship across distance — a base, not a crisis.
- **W3 — the meditation floor goes in.** Daily, small, real — 5 minutes, stacked into the first hour or the wind-down (already half-built into your day). You have the skill; this is re-engagement, not learning. Therapy's first real sessions land here too.
- **W4 (surgery) — the unexpected mental rep.** Being forced off the felt is its own test — the "who am I when I'm not playing" wobble, the patience of recovery. Keep the meditation floor (it doubles as recovery support) and let therapy hold the week. Low pressure, but don't skip the foundation just because poker paused.
- **W5 — gentle return, foundation steady.** Meditation holds, therapy cadence continues. As you ease back online, restart the pre/post-session routines deliberately at low stakes.
- **W6 — line up the performance layer.** Now Layer 2: a mental-game coach or the established material, plus hypnotherapy (which has worked for you) for specific jobs — sleep, reset, focus. Begin the real work: **build your tilt profile** (your specific triggers — the hands, the spots, the player types that get you) and draft the **downswing protocol** while you're *not* in one.
- **W7 — practice under volume + the review line.** The routines get reps as volume rebuilds. The Sunday review now carries a "what tilted me / what I avoided / mood" line (it switches on this week). Therapy + meditation steady.
- **W8 — the real test: the solo dry-run.** When you run the "as if she's away" days, this is the week the *mental* tools get rehearsed, not just the schedule — does the self-regulation hold without her around? Whatever wobbles is exactly what to bring to therapy before Monterey. The Phase 4 danger zone, rehearsed in miniature.
- **W9 — lock the portable versions.** Confirm the whole stack travels: therapy booked through Monterey, meditation floor portable, reset rituals and the downswing protocol written down and ready. You walk into the solo grind with the foundation already running.

The throughline: by the time isolation × variance × duration peaks in Phase 4, none of this is new — it's been running for months. That's the whole reason it lives in Phase 1.
