# Nutrition — the 45 Layer
*The neglected lever, and at 45 the high-leverage one. Goal: lose 30 lbs while preserving muscle. That makes protein and a modest deficit the whole game — aggressive cutting at 45 costs muscle you can't easily rebuild.*

## The principles

1. **Protein first — the single biggest lever.** At 45 your body uses protein less efficiently (anabolic resistance), so it's what protects muscle in the deficit and drives recovery from training. Practical guide: protein with every meal, ~0.7–1 g per lb of goal bodyweight (~110–150 g/day around 150), spread across the day (~30–40 g per meal). Hit it *most* days — a guide, not a tracking prison.

2. **Modest deficit, not aggressive.** The ~1 lb/week rate is right — slow enough to keep muscle. It comes from the defaults + training, **not** from slashing calories. Crash deficits at 45 strip muscle and tank recovery and play.

3. **Defaults, not a meal plan** (same philosophy as the daily plan). A default protein-forward breakfast, decent options stocked so the lazy choice is a fine choice, water at the table. Consistency beats precision.

4. **Fix the poker-lifestyle pattern.** Late nights and odd hours breed the real problem: skip meals → binge → energy drinks → junk at the table. Counter it: don't skip (protein at regular points even on session days), real food stocked for sessions, steady hydration, and the caffeine cutoff — which matters *more* at 45, since caffeine lingers longer and sleep is your keystone.

5. **Recovery + joints (the 45 + injury angle).** Enough protein, whole foods, and omega-3s support the slower recovery and the joint/tendon health your PF/knee history needs. Not prescriptive — just bias toward whole foods and adequacy over restriction.

6. **Alcohol hits harder now.** It blunts recovery and sleep more at 45 than it used to — the sustainable-moderation point matters more, especially around play and the holidays.

## Tracking (lightly)

- A periodic **protein check** — am I hitting it most days?
- **Weight + waist** trend (the scale understates fat loss while you build muscle).
- **Energy and recovery** feel — the real-world readout. Oura readiness backs it up.
- Optionally a short calibration period to learn portions, then default. No obsessive logging.

## How it phases

- **Build phases (1–3):** the modest deficit runs here — most of the 30 lbs comes off (paused for the W4 surgery window — see below). Protein high throughout.
- **Grind (4):** at goal → maintenance. Keep protein high to hold muscle through the volume.
- **WSOP (5):** fuel for performance, not a deficit — steady energy across 12-hour days, avoid the dinner-break crash, hydrate, manage caffeine for the long day without wrecking that night's sleep.
- **Landing (6):** recovery eating, back to home defaults.

## The eating layer on the Phase 1 days

Principles only matter once they sit on the actual day. Here's protein-first mapped onto the playbook's hourly templates — defaults, not a meal plan.

- **Wake / first hour (10:00) — protein breakfast, every day.** The anchor meal: ~30–40 g protein so the day opens *ahead* on the lever instead of chasing it. Lazy-but-good defaults: eggs + whatever's around, Greek yogurt + fruit + nuts, or a shake on a rushed morning. This one meal does most of the work of hitting the daily target.
- **Lunch (~13:00) — the default plate.** Protein + vegetables + a smart carb, same shape most days so it's automatic.
- **Pre-session meal (live nights, ~16:45) — the most important poker meal.** Protein + slower carbs, moderate size, nothing heavy or greasy. Eaten ~90 min before you sit, it does two jobs: steady energy for the long session, and it pre-empts the casino-food default (the nachos-at-midnight trap). Walk in fueled.
- **In-session fuel (live + MTT).** Real food stashed — jerky, nuts, a proper sandwich — and water constant. The job is never getting hungry enough to start the energy-drink-and-junk spiral. On the **Sunday MTT day the dinner-on-a-break is non-negotiable** (it's already in the day template): protein-first, eaten between hands, not skipped.
- **The late-night rule — where eating meets the sleep keystone.** After a 1:00–1:30 finish you'll be hungry, but a big late meal wrecks the sleep the whole system is built on. So **front-load**: eat enough across the pre-session meal and in-session snacks that you reach the wind-down satisfied, not starving. If you genuinely need something after, keep it small and protein-forward (Greek yogurt, cottage cheese, a few slices of turkey) — not a carb-and-grease binge. Protects sleep *and* the deficit in one move.
- **Study / Ellie days (Mon / Thu) — the clean deficit days.** No session to disrupt eating, plus a deliberate shared dinner — the easiest days to eat well and let the modest deficit work. Make the shared dinner protein-first and genuinely good; eating well together is its own anchor.
- **Hydration.** Water throughout every session; on the longest days (8-hour MTT Sundays, late live nights) a zero-sugar electrolyte helps — not an energy drink. Caffeine in before the 18:00 cutoff, as the templates already set.
- **Alcohol at the table.** The live-room default is to drink; yours isn't, on session nights especially. It blunts decisions in the moment, costs sleep after, and stalls the cut — three things you're specifically protecting. Save it for the off, in moderation.

## Around the surgery (W4)

The one window where the deficit pauses — healing needs fuel:

- **No cut during recovery.** Eat to adequacy with **protein high** — protein is what rebuilds tissue. Under-eating right after surgery slows healing; this is the opposite of a restriction week.
- **Hydration + gentle fiber.** Post-op pain meds commonly back things up; water and fiber help. (Follow your surgeon's actual guidance — this isn't medical advice.)
- **Resume gently.** Ease the modest deficit back in from W5–6 as you return to movement, not before.
- **Flag supplements to the surgeon** — the fish oil especially, ahead of the date (already noted in the supplements section).

## Default plates (so the lazy choice is the good one)

Not a meal plan — just the shapes to stock for, so the easy grab is already on target:

- **Breakfast:** eggs / Greek yogurt / a shake — pick a protein, add fruit or veg.
- **Lunch & dinner:** a palm-or-two of protein + a pile of veg + a fist of smart carb (rice, potato, oats, beans).
- **Session snacks:** jerky, nuts, cheese, a pre-made protein sandwich, a shake.
- **Post-session (only if needed):** something small and protein-forward.

Stock these and the deficit mostly takes care of itself — you're not counting, you're defaulting to good.

## The standing shopping list

The kitchen's job is to make the lazy choice a good one — so this is a *standing* list of staples to keep stocked, not a one-off. Build each week's cart from it, swap within categories for what you actually like, and lean toward the no-spoil and pre-cooked options.

**Protein (the anchor of every grab)**
- Eggs (and/or liquid egg whites)
- High-protein Greek yogurt + cottage cheese
- Chicken — fresh breasts/thighs *and* a pre-cooked/rotisserie for lazy nights
- Lean ground beef or turkey
- Fatty fish for the omega-3s (salmon — fresh, frozen, or canned)
- Canned tuna/salmon (shelf-stable backup protein)
- Deli turkey/chicken (sandwiches + the small late-night protein)
- Whey/casein protein powder

**Vegetables (volume + micros)**
- Frozen broccoli / spinach / mixed veg — the no-spoil default
- Bagged salad + whatever fresh veg you'll genuinely eat

**Smart carbs**
- Rice (microwave pouches for lazy days) + oats
- Potatoes / sweet potatoes
- Canned beans / lentils
- Whole-grain bread or wraps

**Session snacks (grab-and-go for Parx + Sunday MTTs)**
- Jerky / biltong
- Mixed nuts, almonds
- String cheese / Babybel
- A couple of decent protein bars

**Fruit + extras**
- Berries, bananas, apples
- Olive oil, avocado, nut butter
- Zero-sugar electrolyte packets
- Flavor with no downside: spices, hot sauce, mustard, salsa

**Lazy-day heroes** (zero cooking, always on hand): rotisserie chicken, microwave rice, canned tuna, Greek yogurt, jerky, a shake. On a brutal day these *are* the meal — and still on target.

*Supplements (creatine, protein, vit D, omega-3, magnesium) are a periodic reorder, not part of the weekly run — see the supplements section.*

## When to shop

**Monday early afternoon — the weekly stock-up.** The cleanest slot in the week: no poker, daytime open, and it lands *before Tuesday's live night*, so the pre-session meal and session snacks are ready when the grind starts. Pair it with ~20 minutes of light prep — hard-boil a few eggs, portion nuts/jerky into session baggies, cook a protein batch — and the grab-and-go is set for the week.

**Optional Thursday top-up** for perishables (more produce, fresh protein) — the other no-poker day, mid-week, so nothing wilts before you use it.

Around the exceptions: in the surgery week (W4) stock up *before* the repair or lean on Ellie; in W1 (Borgata) you're improvising anyway.
