# Partnership Track — being her best partner through her clinical year
*The fourth track. Not in service of the other three — the whole point of a "sustainable life" is that this is the one that holds.*

## The frame

This is Ellie's hard year too. PA clinical rotations are punishing — long hours, a new specialty and often a new city every month (Monterey, Cleveland, Long Island, Rockville), end-of-rotation exams, and the isolation of being the new person everywhere. The job here isn't to manage the relationship around poker. It's to be the steady base she comes home to, and the person who makes her year easier rather than harder.

## The one move that matters most

**Build this with her, not for her.** Before designing anything, have the real conversation: what does she need from you this year, what does support look like when she's slammed, which windows are non-negotiable for the two of you. Everything below is a scaffold to bring to that talk — she edits it. Guessing at what "best partner" means without asking her is the one way to get this wrong.

## The rhythm (the inverse of your grind calendar)

Her availability is the mirror image of when you can grind:

- **When she's on a rotation (Philly or away):** support from a distance. Carry the home and life load so her energy goes to the rotation. Be reachable and low-drama. The *away* rotations are the lonely ones — that's when a call, a visit, or just being present from afar lands hardest.
- **In the free gaps (summer, holidays, the spring breaks):** full presence. Plan them ahead so they don't dissolve into grind — real time together, rest, something to look forward to. The holidays (~Nov 26 – Jan 3) are the big one.
- **Every day, regardless:** one real touchpoint — a shared meal when the schedules cross, a genuine check-in that's connection and not logistics. Steady beats grand.

## Carry the load

The thing that helps most during clinical year is removing friction from her life — logistics, errands, the apartment, the boring admin — so her limited energy isn't spent on chores. Be the reason home is the easy part of her year.

## Show up for the milestones

Her clinical year has real markers — end-of-rotation exams, and **graduation and boards.** With a rotation still TBD *after* the August 2027 one, her clinical year runs past this plan, so graduation lands later — likely fall 2027 or beyond, clear of the WSOP window. Being present for it is a next-cycle item, not a collision with the slate. Still worth pinning the date as it firms up, and being present for the in-year milestones (each end-of-rotation exam, the hard weeks) along the way.

## "Measuring" it (gently)

No metrics — a relationship isn't a leaderboard, and scoring it would miss the point. Instead, a periodic honest check-in, with yourself and with her: am I showing up the way I want to? Is she feeling supported, or like she's doing her hard year alone? Ask her directly rather than inferring. That conversation *is* the measure.
